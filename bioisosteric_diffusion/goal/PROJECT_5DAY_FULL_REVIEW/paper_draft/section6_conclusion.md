## 6. Conclusion

We introduced a benchmark-audit framework for closed-vocabulary scaffold-conditioned fragment replacement ranking. The random/permissive split stress test shows why transform-heldout evaluation is necessary: when transform identities cross train and evaluation partitions, learned ranking signals can appear substantially more transferable than they are under secondary blind evaluation. Under that stricter protocol, candidate-level scoring improves Top-10 recovery, with a prospective 82-feature scorer reaching Top-10 = 0.8851 and a locked post-audit 77-feature scorer reaching Top-10 = 0.9243 after removal of query-relative prior-rank shortcuts.

The 77-feature result should be interpreted as a signal-transfer audit, not as a fully prospective feature-selection claim. It improves Top-10 recovery over Score Blend by Delta Top-10 = +0.0686 and reduces Score Blend hit loss by 5.9x relative to the initial 82-feature scorer, but it is lower on Top-1 and MRR and therefore does not dominate the full ranking profile. A4C provenance strata provide only a coverage-limited computational triage layer, and all labels remain structure-derived rather than activity-preserving endpoints. The general lesson is therefore methodological: remove transform memorization first, then ask which ranking signals still transfer under a locked secondary blind protocol.
