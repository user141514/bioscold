# Leakage-Controlled Closed-Vocabulary Fragment Replacement Ranking with Candidate-Level Scoring

## Abstract

Matched molecular pair (MMP)-derived fragment replacement ranking is useful for studying observed substitution patterns, but permissive splits can leak fragment-attachment transforms and make non-transferable ranking signals look reliable. We present a benchmark-audit framework for closed-vocabulary scaffold-conditioned replacement ranking: first enforce transform-heldout secondary blind evaluation, then test which empirical, structural, and candidate-level signals still transfer. In a random/permissive stress test with 840/840 blind transform identities overlapping training, learned base rankers appeared substantially stronger than under transform-heldout evaluation, motivating the secondary blind protocol. On 13,347 secondary blind queries, a prospective 82-feature candidate-level scorer achieved Top-10 = 0.8851 (95% CI [0.8796, 0.8903]), and a post-audit 77-feature scorer achieved Top-10 = 0.9243 (95% CI [0.9199, 0.9287]) after removing sparse prior-rank features identified as shortcut-like diagnostics. The post-audit scorer improved over Score Blend by Delta Top-10 = 0.0686 (95% CI [0.0638, 0.0733]), but it did not dominate all ranking metrics and remains a locked post-selection result requiring prospective replication. All labels are structure-derived from ChEMBL MMPs and do not establish activity preservation; A4C provenance strata are reported only as unvalidated computational triage signals.
