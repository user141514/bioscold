Figure 2. Candidate-level scoring pipeline and evidence hierarchy. Query-candidate pairs are evaluated by multiple base rankers, whose outputs are combined with train-derived priors, molecular descriptors, and frozen categorical schema features to form a candidate-level feature matrix for HistGB scoring and final ranking. The audit ribbon shows how the initial 82-feature scorer augments the base 77-feature representation with prior-rank features; secondary blind diagnostics identified prior ranks as a non-transferable shortcut, yielding the post-audit locked 77-feature scorer. Evidence tags distinguish the prospective 82-feature scorer from the post-audit locked 77-feature scorer.
