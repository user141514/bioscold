Figure 4. Post-audit prior-rank pruning improves transfer and reduces regression. (A) Leave-one-family-out ablation relative to the initial 82-feature scorer. Removing prior ranks produces the largest positive Top-10 change, whereas removing model ranks or model scores reduces blind performance. The confidence interval is shown only for the prior-rank deletion. (B) Reliability summary after pruning. Lost queries against Score Blend decrease from 596 for the initial 82-feature scorer to 101 for the post-audit 77-feature scorer. The 77-feature scorer rescues 1,016 baseline misses and loses 101 baseline hits relative to Score Blend, and rescues 626 queries while losing 102 relative to the initial 82-feature scorer. The prior-rank deletion was identified after blind diagnostics and is therefore interpreted as a locked post-selection finding rather than a fully prospective feature-selection result.
